The Problem

Rivers are drowning
in devotion

Every day, thousands of kilograms of flowers offered at temples are disposed of in rivers, causing severe water pollution, eutrophication, and ecological damage.

🌊

The Silent Crisis

Over 800 million kilograms of temple waste enters Indian rivers annually. Flowers mixed with chemical dyes, synthetic threads, and plastic packaging devastate aquatic ecosystems and threaten communities that depend on these rivers.

✦
Our Solution

Turning faith into
fragrance

Sughandh Setu intercepts temple waste before it reaches waterways, transforming sacred flowers into premium eco-products through ethical, community-led recycling.

♻️

A Circular Revolution

Every marigold, rose, and jasmine that would have polluted a river is now a stick of incense, a packet of organic color, or nourishing compost — creating value at every step of the chain.

About Us

Built on purpose,
scaled with vision

Sughandh Setu was born from a simple observation — the flowers placed at the feet of deities deserve a sacred end, not a polluted river. We are a for-purpose startup bridging ancient devotion with modern sustainability.

How It Works

From temple to treasure

A meticulously designed four-step system that ensures no sacred flower goes to waste.

01
🪷

Collect

We partner with temples across the city to collect used flowers daily using our dedicated fleet of eco-vehicles. No flower is left behind.

02
🔬

Segregate

Flowers are sorted by type, separated from non-biodegradable materials, and cleaned at our processing centers employing local women.

03
⚗️

Recycle

Using cold-press and bio-extraction techniques, we extract natural fragrance compounds, pigments, and biomass — zero chemical solvents.

04
✨

Create

The extracted essences become premium dhoop batti, agarbatti, organic gulal, and compost — ready for homes, markets, and gift shelves.
